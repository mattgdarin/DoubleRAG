Acme Corp Overview
Acme Corp was founded in 2008 by Sandra Okafor and James Whitfield in Austin, Texas.
The company specializes in industrial water filtration systems for municipal and commercial clients.
As of 2024, Acme Corp employs 340 full-time staff across three offices: Austin (HQ), Denver, and Toronto.

Products and Services
Acme Corp's flagship product is the ClearFlow 3000, a modular filtration unit capable of processing up to 500,000 gallons per day.
The ClearFlow 3000 uses a patented three-stage filtration process: sediment removal, activated carbon treatment, and UV sterilization.
Acme also offers the ClearFlow Lite for small commercial clients, processing up to 50,000 gallons per day.
Installation, maintenance contracts, and 24/7 emergency support are bundled into Acme's ServiceShield plans.

Financials
Acme Corp reported $87 million in revenue for fiscal year 2023, up 14% from the prior year.
Their largest revenue segment is recurring maintenance contracts, which account for 61% of total revenue.
The company operates on a gross margin of approximately 42%.
Acme closed a $25 million Series C funding round in early 2024 led by BlueWater Ventures.

Clients and Markets
Acme's top clients include the City of Denver Water Authority, a pharmaceutical manufacturer in Ontario, and three major hotel chains.
The company primarily serves clients in the United States and Canada, with early-stage expansion into Mexico underway.
Municipal contracts typically run 5-7 years and are renegotiated based on performance benchmarks.

Operations
Acme's Austin headquarters houses the R&D team, which consists of 45 engineers focused on next-generation filtration membranes.
The Denver office manages sales and field operations for the western US.
The Toronto office handles Canadian client relationships and regulatory compliance.
Acme has a preferred supplier agreement with FilterTech Inc. for activated carbon media, renewed annually.

Competitors
Acme's main competitors are PureWave Systems, HydroShield, and municipal in-house operations.
PureWave is larger, with approximately $210 million in revenue, but operates primarily in the Southeast US.
Acme differentiates on modular design, faster installation time (typically 6 weeks vs. industry average of 14), and ServiceShield's guaranteed response times.

Upcoming Initiatives
Acme plans to launch the ClearFlow 5000 in Q3 2025, targeting large-scale industrial clients processing over 2 million gallons per day.
The company is exploring a partnership with a European distributor to enter the German and Dutch markets by 2026.
An internal AI monitoring system called AquaWatch is in development, designed to predict filter degradation and automate maintenance scheduling.
